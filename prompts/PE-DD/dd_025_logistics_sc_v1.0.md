## ENGINE 1 · SUPPLY CHAIN RISK MAPPER (SCRM)

```
FUNCTION map_supply_chain_risk(network_data, customer_data, geo_data):

  STEP 1 — NETWORK TOPOLOGY ANALYSIS:
    Node Classification:
      Tier-1 Nodes: Major hubs (port terminals, large DCs, airport cargo facilities)
      Tier-2 Nodes: Regional DCs, cross-dock facilities, rail intermodal yards
      Tier-3 Nodes: Last-mile depots, pickup points, micro-fulfillment centers

    Network Resilience Metrics:
      Hub-and-Spoke redundancy: # alternate routing paths per critical node
      Single-point-of-failure (SPOF): nodes with 0 alternate routes = CRITICAL FLAG
      Network utilization: peak vs. average; surge capacity headroom (%)
      Intermodal flexibility: modal substitution capability (road/rail/sea/air)

    Chokepoint Dependency (Z-13):
      Global choke: Suez / Panama / Strait of Malacca / Taiwan Strait dependency score
      Regional choke: single border crossing / port pair concentration
      Corridor concentration: single trade lane >50% of volume → Z-13 FLAG
      China-origin dependency: % of total volume from China-origin suppliers
        >60%: HIGH → friend-shoring stress test required
        30~60%: MODERATE → China+1 diversification trajectory assessment
        <30%: LOW → acceptable baseline

  STEP 2 — SUPPLIER & CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION:
    Supplier concentration (upstream):
      Single-supplier >40% of critical input → E-18 + Z-13 FLAG
      Sole-source components: # critical SKUs with 0 alternate suppliers
      Geographic supplier cluster: >60% from single country → Z-10 escalation

    Customer concentration (downstream) — E-05:
      Top-1 shipper revenue %: >30% → KEY CUSTOMER FLAG
      Top-3 shipper revenue %: >50% → ELEVATED; retention analysis mandatory
      Contract duration: spot (<1yr) vs. contract (1~3yr) vs. long-term (>3yr)
      Dedicated network customer: exit barrier analysis; volume commitment clause

  STEP 3 — DISRUPTION SCENARIO MODELING (Z-13):
    Scenario A — Port Closure (Tier-1 node outage):
      Volume rerouting: alternate port capacity; transit time delta
      Revenue impact: volume loss × margin × duration; customer SLA breach cost
      Recovery timeline: 30/60/90-day scenario

    Scenario B — Trade War / Tariff Escalation:
      China-origin tariff stress: +25% / +50% / full ban scenario
      Reshoring cost: volume shift to Mexico/Vietnam/India → capex requirement
      Lane profitability under tariff: contribution margin erosion

    Scenario C — Pandemic / Black Swan:
      Volume collapse: -30% / -50% scenario; fixed cost coverage
      Network mothballing: reversibility of asset base; lease obligation tail
      E-commerce surge demand: fulfillment surge capacity vs. normal capacity

    Scenario D — Fuel/Energy Price Shock:
      Bunker fuel: +50% / +100% → ocean freight P&L impact (pass-through rate)
      Road: diesel +50% → trucking cost; EV fleet conversion buffer
      Air cargo: jet fuel surcharge mechanism; contractual pass-through %

  SCRM_SCORE (0~10):
    Network redundancy       (0~2.5)
    Supplier diversification (0~2.5)
    Customer concentration   (0~2.5)
    Disruption resilience    (0~2.5)
  THRESHOLD: <5 → Z-13 mandatory deep-dive; No-Go pending resilience plan

  RETURN: {spof_count, corridor_concentration, china_dependency_pct,
           customer_hhi, disruption_resilience_score, scrm_score}
```

## ENGINE 2 · TRADE LANE ANALYZER (TLA)

```
FUNCTION analyze_trade_lanes(lane_data, rate_data, regulatory_data):

  MODULE A — FREIGHT RATE & MARKET POSITIONING:
    Ocean Freight:
      Spot rate index: Drewry WCI / Freightos FBX / CCFI (China export)
      Contract rate: % of volume on long-term contract vs. spot; hedge ratio
      Spot/contract spread: current vs. 3-yr average; rate cycle positioning
      VSA (Vessel Sharing Agreement): alliance membership; capacity access
      Laden/empty ratio: vessel utilization by lane; repositioning cost

    Air Freight:
      IATA Cargo iQ / CLIVE rate index: belly vs. freighter yield split
      e-AWB penetration: digitization level (IATA target 100%)
      Express premium: B2C vs. B2B cargo mix; time-sensitivity premium

    Road (FTL/LTL):
      DAT spot rate / Coyote index: lane-level rate vs. market
      Driver availability: CDL driver shortage metric (US: 80k+ deficit)
      Fuel surcharge: indexed mechanism (DOE national avg); pass-through %
      Backhaul optimization: empty mile %; target <15%

    Rail / Intermodal:
      Class I railroad congestion: velocity metric; dwell time
      Port-rail drayage: first-mile efficiency; chassis availability
      China-Europe rail (Belt & Road): Z-10 / Z-13 geopolitical assessment

  MODULE B — LANE PROFITABILITY:
    Revenue per lane: total revenue / # active lanes
    Contribution margin by lane:
      >15%: PREMIUM lane | 8~15%: CORE | 3~8%: MARGINAL | <3%: BELOW THRESHOLD → review
    Volume density: shipments per lane per week; minimum viable density
    Lane EBITDA: revenue - direct transport cost - handling - overhead allocation
    Cross-selling: customs brokerage attach rate; insurance; SCF referral

  MODULE C — REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AUDIT (E-16):
    Customs compliance:
      Importer Security Filing (ISF) / Advance Manifest: filing accuracy rate
      CBP audit history: liquidation rate; penalty frequency
      AEO / C-TPAT certification: security premium; border facilitation benefit
    Export compliance:
      EAR/ITAR screening: automated denied-party screening (DPS) system
      AES/SED filing accuracy: SED error rate; voluntary self-disclosure history
      Transshipment monitoring: country-of-origin verification; diversion risk
    Trade Sanctions:
      OFAC SDN / EU consolidated list: real-time screening frequency
      Russia/Iran/DPRK cargo: zero-tolerance protocol; counterparty chain check
      BIS Entity List: tech component in cargo: export license check

  TLA_SCORE (0~10):
    Rate positioning vs. market  (0~2.5)
    Lane profitability quality   (0~2.5)
    Regulatory compliance        (0~2.5)
    Geo-risk adjusted lanes      (0~2.5)

  RETURN: {lane_count, avg_contribution_margin, spot_contract_ratio,
           compliance_score, geo_adjusted_revenue_pct, tla_score}
```

## ENGINE 4 · SUPPLY CHAIN FINANCE ENGINE (SCFE)

FUNCTION assess_sc_finance(financials, working_capital, contract_data):

  MODULE A — REVENUE QUALITY:
    Revenue Composition:
      Contractual base revenue: % on multi-year contracts (>1yr) → stability premium
      Spot/transactional revenue: cyclical exposure; rate volatility pass-through
      Value-added services (VAS): customs, insurance, SCF, labeling, kitting
        VAS revenue % >20%: PREMIUM (sticky, higher margin)
      Fuel surcharge pass-through: indexed mechanism: ≥80% pass-through = INSULATED
      Currency pass-through: contract FX mechanism; natural hedge from multi-currency ops

    Revenue per unit economics:
      Revenue/TEU (ocean) | Revenue/lb (air) | Revenue/shipment (CEP)
      Trend: expanding / stable / contracting vs. prior 3yr; cycle adjustment
      Yield management: dynamic pricing adoption; algorithmic vs. manual

  MODULE B — WORKING CAPITAL DYNAMICS:
    DSO (Days Sales Outstanding): Logistics benchmark 35~45 days
      >60 days: customer credit risk flag; factoring dependency
      <30 days: EXCELLENT; prepaid/upfront contract benefit
    DPO (Days Payable Outstanding): carrier / subcontractor payment terms
      <30 days: cash conversion pressure
      30~60 days: standard; optimal working capital
    CCC (Cash Conversion Cycle = DSO + DIO - DPO):
      Negative CCC: platform/4PL premium → capital-light model benefit
      Positive CCC >45 days: working capital financing need
    Advance rate / Supply Chain Finance:
      SCF platform: buyer-confirmed payables; early payment discount yield
      Inventory financing: ABL facility; advance rate vs. liquidation value
      Revenue factoring (if applicable): recourse vs. non-recourse; cost of capital

  MODULE C — PROFITABILITY BENCHMARKS:
    Gross Margin by Segment:
      Asset-Heavy 3PL: 15~25% | Asset-Light brokerage: 12~20%
      4PL / Control Tower: 8~15% (lower gross, higher EBITDA on capital-light)
      Cold Chain: 20~35% (premium for temperature-controlled infrastructure)
      Port Terminal: 35~55% EBITDA margin (regulated concession premium)
      CEP / Express: 10~20% gross (volume-driven; last-mile cost intensive)
      SCM SaaS: 60~75% gross margin (software benchmark)
    EBITDA Margin:
      Best-in-class asset-heavy 3PL: 10~14%
      Asset-light/4PL: 12~18%
      Port terminal: 40~55%
      Tech-enabled platform: 15~30%
    EBITDA to FCF conversion: CapEx intensity;
      Asset-heavy: FCF conversion 50~65% (high fleet capex)
      Asset-light: FCF conversion 75~90% (capital-light)
      Port terminal: FCF conversion 60~75% (concession capex; maintenance)

  MODULE D — VALUATION FRAMEWORK:
    Comparable Transaction Multiples (2023~2026 PE/M&A):
      Asset-Heavy 3PL: EV/EBITDA 8~12× | EV/Revenue 0.5~1.0×
      Asset-Light / Digital Freight Brokerage: EV/EBITDA 10~16× | EV/GP 6~10×
      4PL / Control Tower: EV/EBITDA 12~18× (tech premium)
      Cold Chain Logistics: EV/EBITDA 12~16× (infrastructure scarcity)
      Port Terminal (concession): EV/EBITDA 14~22× | DCF-driven; concession life
      Last-Mile / CEP: EV/EBITDA 8~14× | E-commerce growth premium
      SCM SaaS: ARR multiple 4~12× (growth-stage); EV/EBITDA 20~35× (profitable)
      Freight Forwarding: EV/EBITDA 6~10× (cyclical; rate-sensitive)
    ESG Premium: Decarbonized fleet / green DC certification: +1~2× EBITDA turn
    Geopolitical Discount: >40% China-origin volume: -1~3× EBITDA turn (Z-13)

  SCFE_SCORE (0~10):
    Revenue quality & stability  (0~2.5)
    Working capital efficiency   (0~2.5)
    Profitability vs. benchmark  (0~2.5)
    Valuation reasonableness     (0~2.5)

  RETURN: {contract_revenue_pct, vas_revenue_pct, ccc_days, ebitda_margin,
